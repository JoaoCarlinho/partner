# Debtor Quick-Start Guide

**Time to read: 5 minutes**

Welcome. We understand receiving a demand letter can be stressful. This platform is designed to help you understand your situation and find a path forward that works for you.

---

## You Have Options

Before anything else, know this: **you have options**. This platform helps you:

- **Understand** what you owe and why
- **Explore** your choices (pay, dispute, negotiate)
- **Communicate** with the creditor in a supportive environment
- **Create** a payment plan that fits your situation

---

## Getting Started

### Step 1: Use Your Invitation Link

You received a letter with a unique link. Click it to:
1. Verify your identity (simple confirmation)
2. Create a secure account
3. Access your case information

### Step 2: View Your Dashboard

Once logged in, your dashboard shows:

- **What You Owe** - Total amount with breakdown
- **Who It's From** - Original creditor information
- **Key Dates** - Response deadline and timeline
- **Your Options** - What you can do next

---

## Understanding Your Dashboard

### Amount Owed Card

Shows the breakdown:
```
Total Amount:     $2,450.00
├─ Original:      $2,000.00
├─ Interest:        $300.00
└─ Fees:            $150.00
```

### Timeline Card

- When the debt originated
- Response deadline (with countdown)
- Urgency indicator

### Your Options

| Option | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Pay** | Pay the full amount or set up payments |
| **Dispute** | Question if the debt is valid |
| **Negotiate** | Propose a payment plan that works for you |
| **Get Help** | Connect with a public defender |

---

## Plain English Version

We've translated your demand letter into simple language:

1. Look for the blue banner: "Plain English version available"
2. Click "View simplified version"
3. Read the letter without legal jargon

## If You Need Help

### Financial Assessment

The platform can help you:
1. Understand your financial situation
2. Find a realistic payment option
3. No judgment - just helpful guidance

### Public Defender

If you want free legal help:
1. A public defender can be assigned
2. They'll review your situation
3. Help you understand your rights

## Key Things to Know

- **Your rights are protected** - You can dispute the debt
- **Time matters** - Note your response deadline
- **You're not alone** - Help is available
- **This is private** - Your information is secure
